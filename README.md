# Nathan Addley Stack

**Visual artist, gallery operations professional, and AI workflow builder.**

I build practical systems for creative work: dashboards, research workflows, portfolio organization, content support, gallery operations, finance visibility, and human reviewed AI tools. This GitHub profile is a public map of that work. Most source code stays private because it touches personal operations, collector data, finance records, client style workflows, credentials, or production infrastructure. The goal here is transparency without exposing private wiring.

### Finance CSV Workflow

A privacy first finance workflow that uses downloaded CSV files instead of bank logins or paid Plaid access.

**Subsystems:**

- CSV inbox and normalized transaction database.
- Category summaries.
- Weekly spending review.
- Emergency buffer tracking.
- Safe to spend style summaries.

**Public boundary:** summaries and architecture can be described. Raw transaction rows, balances, bank files, and account details stay private.

### Trading Research Lane

A locked and research first trading lane designed for paper analysis, discipline, and risk management rather than uncontrolled execution.

**Subsystems:**

- Riley for paper only memecoin research and watchlist logic.
- Huey for future risk controls, currently disabled for live signing.
- Strict separation between research, simulation, and any real trading action.
- Loss control rules, stop loss logic, and exit ladder tracking.

**Public boundary:** this is presented as risk workflow and research design. It is not a claim of autonomous live trading.

## Selected art and creative work context

My painting practice includes large scale oil and acrylic works centered on Black American life, memory, portraiture, dignity, and the transformation of everyday scenes into symbolic spaces. Recent bodies of work include the Black cowboy and horse rider paintings, which explore erased histories, power, leisure, myth, movement, and contemporary Black self possession.

- System descriptions.
- Safe demo links.

- Non sensitive project summaries.
- Sanitized screenshots and proof packets.

**Private:**

## Current direction

The goal is to build a serious creative operating system that supports art making, gallery work, collector relationships, finances, research, content support, and business development without mixing private information into public code. The work is both artistic and infrastructural: paintings, systems, workflows, and tools built with the same level of intention.
