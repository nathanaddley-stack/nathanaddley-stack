# Nathan Addley Stack

**Public system map for Nathan Addley: visual artist, gallery operations professional, and creative systems builder.**

This page is maintained by Nathan's AI agent team, mainly Luke on the Hermes side and Anakin on the OpenClaw side, under Nathan's direction. Nathan provides the goals, standards, approvals, corrections, and real-world context. The agents help turn that direction into organized workflows, documentation, demos, and technical structure.

Most source code stays private because the system touches personal operations, collector relationships, finance records, gallery administration, client-style workflows, private notes, credentials, and agent infrastructure. This public repo exists to show the architecture, creative workflow thinking, and privacy boundaries without exposing sensitive wiring.

### Finance CSV Workflow

A privacy-first finance workflow that uses downloaded CSV files instead of bank logins or paid Plaid access.

**Subsystems:**

- CSV inbox and normalized transaction database.
- Category summaries.
- Weekly spending review.
- Emergency buffer tracking.
- Safe-to-spend style summaries.

**Public boundary:** summaries and architecture can be described. Raw transaction rows, balances, bank files, and account details stay private.

### Trading Research Lane

A locked, research-first trading lane designed for paper analysis, discipline, and risk management rather than uncontrolled execution.

**Subsystems:**

- Riley for paper-only memecoin research and watchlist logic.
- Huey for future risk controls, currently disabled for live signing.
- Strict separation between research, simulation, and any real trading action.
- Loss control rules, stop-loss logic, and exit ladder tracking.

**Public boundary:** this is presented as risk workflow and research design. It is not a claim of autonomous live trading.

### Meme Coin Studio and Culture Research

A creative research lane focused on culture, visual identity, market behavior, and storytelling around internet-native projects.

**Subsystems:**

- Internet culture notes.
- Visual identity critique.
- Paper calls and simulated entries.
- Project narrative research.
- Public build logs and social strategy concepts.

## Selected art and creative work context

Nathan's painting practice includes large-scale oil and acrylic works centered on Black American life, memory, portraiture, dignity, and the transformation of everyday scenes into symbolic spaces. Recent bodies of work include the Black cowboy and horse rider paintings, which explore erased histories, power, leisure, myth, movement, and contemporary Black self-possession.

Nathan's professional background also includes commissions for institutions such as SCAD and private clients including Shannon McCollum, father of Lil Yachty, along with exhibition and sales experience through platforms connected to SCAD, ZuCot Gallery, and Black Art in America.
