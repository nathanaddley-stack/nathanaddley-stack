# Nathan Addley Stack

Artist, painter, gallery operations professional, and systems builder working across fine art, creative operations, automation, and AI assisted workflows.

This profile is a public map of the systems and subsystems I am building. Most code stays private because it connects to personal operations, client workflows, finance records, collector data, gallery administration, and agent infrastructure. The purpose of this page is to show the architecture clearly without exposing private wiring.

## Core identity

- Atlanta based artist and MFA candidate in Painting at the Savannah College of Art and Design
- Contemporary painter focused on Black American life, dignity, memory, resilience, portraiture, and the human experience
- Gallery operations professional working across art handling, installation support, inventory, shipping, social media, collector presentation, and art fair booth setup
- Builder of personal AI systems for planning, research, creative operations, finance organization, portfolio support, and workflow automation

## System map

### Life Desk

A personal operating system built to organize the moving parts of art, money, projects, research, and daily decisions.

Focus areas:

- Art practice and professional art career
- Finance tracking and weekly spending visibility
- Project planning and business development
- Research, writing, and opportunity scouting
- Safe separation between active systems and experimental systems

### Luke

Chief of staff style assistant system for personal context, planning, art career administration, memory, reminders, writing, and plain English summaries.

Subsystems:

- Art career desk
- Collector and gallery relationship notes
- Resume, CV, Behance, LinkedIn, and portfolio writing support
- Finance CSV summaries and budget review
- Obsidian based knowledge vault
- Memory health and profile organization

### Anakin and OpenClaw

Technical operator lane for code, repositories, deployments, VPS work, dashboards, services, and infrastructure.

Subsystems:

- Command Center UI
- OpenClaw gateway and agent orchestration
- Repo backup and deployment workflows
- App routing and production verification
- Local memory and embedding infrastructure

### Art Career Desk

A structured art career workflow for turning scattered opportunities into organized next actions.

Subsystems:

- Collector outreach and follow up drafts
- Available works and artwork metadata
- Gallery, curator, and buyer research
- Opportunity scouting for grants, residencies, exhibitions, and open calls
- Portfolio, CV, artist statement, and profile materials
- Art letter list and lead mining from email history

### Gallery Operations and Creative Systems

Workflow systems built around real gallery work and creative administration.

Areas of work:

- Art handling and installation support
- Inventory and artwork documentation
- Packing, shipping, and presentation prep
- Social media and creative content support
- Art fair booth setup and exhibition preparation
- AI assisted workflow automation for organizing information, improving documentation, and reducing repetitive operations work

### DotDat Intelligence Demo

A proposal and intelligence demo for showing how structured research, prompt routing, and safe response design can support business development.

Focus areas:

- Professional demo interface
- Safer off topic handling
- Research and proposal support
- Public preview with private source backup

### Finance CSV Workflow

A privacy first finance workflow that uses downloaded CSV files instead of bank logins or paid Plaid access.

Subsystems:

- CSV inbox and normalized transaction database
- Category summaries
- Weekly spending review
- Emergency buffer tracking
- Safe to spend style summaries

### Trading Research Lane

A locked and research first trading lane designed for paper analysis, discipline, and risk management rather than uncontrolled execution.

Subsystems:

- Riley for paper only memecoin research and watchlist logic
- Huey for future risk controls, currently disabled for live signing
- Strict separation between research, simulation, and any real trading action
- Loss control rules, stop loss logic, and exit ladder tracking

### Meme Coin Studio and Culture Research

A creative research lane focused on culture, visual identity, market behavior, and storytelling around internet native projects.

Subsystems:

- Internet culture notes
- Visual identity critique
- Paper calls and simulated entries
- Project narrative research
- Public build logs and social strategy concepts

## Selected art and creative work context

My painting practice includes large scale oil and acrylic works centered on Black American life, memory, portraiture, dignity, and the transformation of everyday scenes into symbolic spaces. Recent bodies of work include the Black cowboy and horse rider paintings, which explore erased histories, power, leisure, myth, movement, and contemporary Black self possession.

My professional background also includes commissions for institutions such as SCAD and private clients including Shannon McCollum, father of Lil Yachty, along with exhibition and sales experience through platforms connected to SCAD, ZuCot Gallery, and Black Art in America.

## What is public versus private

Public:

- High level architecture
- System descriptions
- Demo links when safe
- Portfolio and professional positioning
- Non sensitive project summaries

Private:

- Collector contact data
- Raw financial records
- Credentials and sensitive access files
- Personal memory dumps and private logs
- Production wiring and sensitive automation details

## Current direction

The goal is to build a serious creative operating system that supports art making, gallery work, collector relationships, finances, research, and business development without mixing private information into public code. The work is both artistic and infrastructural: paintings, systems, workflows, and tools built with the same level of intention.
